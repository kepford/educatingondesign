Educating on Design

a symposium on educating future web designers

What is it?

A day-long symposium for educators and web designers to come together for talks about design principals, industry skills and standards as it applies to preparing students for careers in design.

Who should attend

This symposium is geared toward high school and college instructors in the areas of web design, graphic design, technology, information technology, video/multimedia, and marketing. Designers, developers, and students are also invited to attend in an effort to open up the conversation between the education and business sides of the design community.

Speakers

  • Samantha Warren

    Samantha Warren loves big concepts as much as she loves badass typography and thrives on telling interesting stories through usable interfaces. She has written articles for .net Magazine, regularly speaks at industry events and is on the Board of the Art Directors Club of Washington DC. When she is not doing any of the above you can find her enthusiastically teaching typography and web design at the Center for Digital Imaging Arts at Boston University.

  • Layne Lev

    Layne has been involved in both the web and print sides of the graphic arts industry for over fourteen years. Prior to the formation of Hundred10, he ran e:go Consulting, an independent design studio servicing clients in both central and northern California. He also served as Creative Director for HiS Magazine and Premier Bride. He holds a Bachelor of Science in Graphic Communication from Cal Poly, San Luis Obispo.

  • Katrina Neufeld

    An award-winning graphic designer, Katrina launched Neu Design in 2000. She holds a BA in graphic design from California State University, Fresno. With a keen eye for detail, Katrina's style is clean, fresh, and intuitive. She offers a unique combination of creativity and technological expertise.
